XeeAI: Integrating Explainable AI Visualization through LIME Algorithm in an AI System

Abong, John Aiverson*, Justinano, Janna Andrea*, Lacap, Xynil Jhed*, Mercado, Raphael *,

Paper Supplementary Code arXiv

Abstract

As artificial intelligence becomes increasingly integrated into high-stakes decision-making systems, the demand for transparency and interpretability continues to grow. Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI) seeks to address this need by making black-box models understandable to human users. This study introduces XeeAI, a chatbot system built using a pre-trained Large Language Model, augmented with Local Interpretable Model-Agnostic Explanations (LIME) to provide token-level visual explanations. The primary focus is to evaluate how XAI techniques like LIME influence user trust and understanding in AI-driven systems, especially proprietary and close-sourced models. Grounded in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) principles and the three-dimensional trust framework (Trustor, Trustee, Interactive Context), the study employs a quasi-experimental design involving pre- and post-test surveys, A/B testing, and ISO 25010 quality evaluation and System Usability Scale. The system incorporates user interface design and usability testing to examine how XAI visualizations affect usersâ€™ trust. Results indicate that users exposed to LIME-enhanced explanations reported improved comprehension, confidence, and perceived system transparency. This work underscores the essential role of explainability in deploying responsible and trustworthy AI applications, especially in contexts where users must understand or validate the AI's responses.
